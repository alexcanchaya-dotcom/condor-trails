Your Journey

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Three weeks across Peru, Bolivia, Argentina, Chile, Ecuador, and Colombia — the ultimate South American adventure.

1–2

Lima — Capital of Cuisine

Peru

Arrive in Lima and immerse yourself in South America's culinary capital. Explore the Larco Museum, the bohemian Barranco district, and enjoy a welcome dinner at a top cevichería. Day two includes a Peruvian cooking class and time to explore Miraflores.

3–4

Cusco & Machu Picchu

Peru

Fly to Cusco, the ancient Inca capital. Explore the Sacred Valley, visit Ollantaytambo, and take the iconic train to Machu Picchu for a sunrise guided tour of the Lost City. Return to Cusco to explore Sacsayhuamán and the artisan quarter.

5–6

Lake Titicaca & La Paz

Bolivia

Cross Lake Titicaca from Peru into Bolivia, visiting the floating Uros islands and Isla del Sol. Continue to La Paz — the world's highest administrative capital — and ride the spectacular Mi Teleférico cable car network above the canyon city.

7–8

Salar de Uyuni & Coloured Lagoons

Bolivia

Fly to Uyuni and drive onto the world's largest salt flat — an infinite white canvas stretching to the horizon. Visit Isla Incahuasi with its giant cacti, then venture into the Eduardo Avaroa Reserve with its coloured lagoons, flamingos, and geysers.

9–11

Buenos Aires & Iguaú Falls

Argentina

Fly to Buenos Aires for tango, steak, and the colourful La Boca neighbourhood. Explore the elegant Recoleta district and San Telmo's antique markets. Then fly north to the thundering Iguaú Falls — one of the world's great natural wonders, viewed from both the Argentine and Brazilian sides.

12–14

Patagonia — Glaciers & Peaks

Patagonia

Fly to El Calafate and witness the mighty Perito Moreno Glacier calving into Lago Argentino. Continue to the trekking capital of El Chaltén for hikes beneath the granite spires of Fitz Roy and Cerro Torre. Three days of Patagonia's most dramatic landscapes.

15–17

Ecuador — Quito & the Highlands

Ecuador

Fly to Quito, a UNESCO World Heritage city perched at 2,850 metres. Explore the magnificent colonial Old Town, visit the famous Otavalo indigenous market — one of the largest in South America — and venture to the base of the Cotopaxi volcano.

18–19

Ecuador — Adventure & Hot Springs

Ecuador

Journey to the adventure town of Baños, perched between the Andes and the Amazon basin. Hike to waterfalls, soak in volcanic hot springs, cross spectacular suspension bridges, and ride the famous "Swing at the End of the World" overlooking the Tungurahua volcano.

20–21

Cartagena — Grand Finale

Colombia

Fly to Cartagena for the perfect finale. Wander the cobblestoned Walled City with its balconies draped in bougainvillea, explore the Getseamí neighbourhood's vibrant street art, enjoy Caribbean seafood by the ramparts, and bid farewell to South America in style. Departure on Day 21.
